research

Research on Jupiter has revealed fascinating insights into its composition, atmosphere, magnetic field, and moons.

Scientists have determined that Jupiter is primarily composed of hydrogen and helium, along with trace elements. Its dynamic atmosphere displays storms, cloud formations, and distinctive banding patterns. The enduring Great Red Spot, a massive storm, captivates researchers studying its origins and atmospheric dynamics.

Jupiter's magnetic field generates radiation belts and auroras, providing valuable insights into its interactions with the surrounding space environment. The field's relationship with moons like Io adds further depth to our understanding.
